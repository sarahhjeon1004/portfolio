AI Trust & Transparency Study
Usability Study Q2 2025 Fusion Enterprise 12 highlights 3 notes 2 insights
TL;DR
Enterprise customers want to trust AI but need transparency to do so. The study found that users will not share AI-generated outputs with clients unless they can verify how the output was produced. The main asks: explainability ("show me why"), audit trails, and the ability to override AI suggestions. Workflow integration matters more than feature sophistication.
Key Findings
"Enterprise customers expressed concern about not understanding how AI generates design recommendations. Several participants said they would not share AI outputs with clients without manual verification."
"The expectation is that AI should explain itself — not just give an answer but show why it arrived there."
"Three of five participants mentioned wanting an 'audit trail' for AI suggestions in their design workflow."
Who should see this
PMs — prioritize explainability in AI feature roadmaps
Engineers — add audit trail logging to AI outputs
Designers — design transparent AI result UIs
Leadership — enterprise AI adoption depends on trust
Study details
Method: Moderated usability study
Participants: 5 enterprise architects (Fusion)
Duration: 60-min sessions
Researcher: UX Research Team

Executive Brief — Q1 2026

Across 38 active studies and 2,450+ research items, three strategic themes dominate this quarter:

1. AI trust is the adoption bottleneck. Enterprise users want AI features but won't use them in client-facing work without explainability. This is the #1 barrier to weekly active usage (23% vs. 67% trial rate).

2. Search and navigation need a rethink. Cross-product search confusion drives users to category browsing. 34% of searches return zero results. Saved filters are the #2 feature request.

3. Developer experience is declining. SDK setup time increased 50% last quarter. API error messages are the top complaint. Platform team investment needed.

Biggest gap: Civil 3D and SMB segments are significantly understudied. 90% of research is AMER-only — EMEA/APAC perspectives are missing.

Why we're building this

Autodesk is a huge company with hundreds of products and decades of deep experience and understanding of our industries — locked away in silos across the company.

The Autodesk-wide research practice core team has been exploring how to unlock our various research repositories for years, so we can ask anything of the data we already have, quickly find out what we know, and invest in researching new things.

With tools like Dovetail, we can now search and summarize hundreds of research projects and customer conversations in minutes and jump right to the sources to learn more. But not everyone or every team has access to Dovetail.

What if we could bring that experience to common places like Slack and Confluence, and drive business and research intelligence into the hands of each curious person?

The problem today

Research is siloed
Dovetail, Prism, PDMS, analytics dashboards, and UPF Hub each hold pieces of the picture. No single place to search across all of them.
Access is limited
Only researchers with Dovetail licenses can search qualitative data. PMs, designers, engineers, and leadership are locked out.
Duplicate research
Teams commission new studies on topics that have already been researched — because they can't find what already exists.
Quantitative + qualitative are disconnected
UPF Hub shows what sentiment looks like. Dovetail and Prism show why. Today you can't connect them.

What Ask Me Anything does

Ask Me Anything is an AI-powered MCP server that connects to all of Autodesk's research repositories and makes them searchable, summarizable, and accessible through tools people already use.

Cross-source search — Ask a question in natural language, get answers from Dovetail, Prism, PDMS, Analytics, and UPF Hub in one summary
Slack bot — Type /ama in any channel to search research without leaving Slack
Confluence sidebar — See related research automatically while reading any Confluence page
Topic alerts — Watch topics and get Slack notifications with AI summaries when new research matches
Chrome extension — Get contextual research on any internal page (Jira, Confluence, Google Docs)
UPF Hub integration — Combine sentiment trends with qualitative research to see the full picture

Model Derivative API — v2 Migration Guide

This page documents the migration path from Model Derivative API v1 to v2. Teams should plan for migration by Q3 2026.

Breaking changes

The POST /modelderivative/v2/designdata/job endpoint replaces the v1 translation workflow. Response format changes include nested manifest objects and new status codes for partial failures.

Known issues

Large Revit models (>500MB) may experience timeout errors during translation. The team is investigating file translation performance for complex assemblies. Error messages for failed translations are being improved based on developer feedback.

Timeline

v1 deprecation: September 2026. All integrations must migrate by this date. See the API changelog for incremental updates.
