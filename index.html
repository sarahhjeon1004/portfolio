01
Autodesk KPI Framework & Visibility System

Impact HQ

A visibility layer for 50+ team members — KPI framework, AI writing coach, and leadership reporting that made research impact measurable without adding burden.

The Problem

Work was happening everywhere across PSET XD — 53 active contributors, 22 reporting cycles — but there was no consistent way to see what was landing and what was blocked. Leadership needed a concise, reliable view of impact. Not more metrics. Not more meetings.

53 Active contributors
~19% Never submitted
60-70% Confused about "impact"
What I Built
Submission Framework

Structured into Impact, Highlight, and Challenge — each with clear purpose and golden examples. "No pressure" messaging throughout.

AI Writing Coach

Stage-aware prompts that move from activity to outcome framing. Validates strong submissions without over-coaching. Polish, not inflation.

KPI Framework

Airtable + shared schema for 50+ people. Monthly leadership digests. One source of truth — governance, not evaluation.

Dashboard Redesign

Removed per-person charts and averages. Added Blockers, Top Highlights, Impact by Theme, and AI-generated executive summaries.

Process
1
Discovery

Usability survey (70% response). Interviews with 5 leaders, researchers, power users. Mapped pain points across timing, impact clarity, and feedback loops.

2
Stakeholder Input

Leadership wanted a digest, not a dashboard. Connie pushed for AI rewrite + summarize. Valerie and Michelle shaped stage-awareness and calibration.

3
Audit & Build

Full system audit — submission flow, report design, dashboard, schema. Structured submission types, AI coach prompt, automation design.

4
Phased Rollout

Phase 1: submission + AI coach + reports. Phase 2: leadership dashboard + impact categorization. Get comfort right before adding complexity.

Impact
50+ People in one visibility system
264 Impact submissions across 22 cycles
~81% Participation rate
↓ Shorter digest cycles, less chasing
Actionable digests replaced ad hoc updates
AI coach improved clarity and consistency
Teams celebrating each other's work
Comparison pressure removed entirely

Research Intake Pipeline

From Confluence chaos to a self-governing Airtable pipeline — where the requestor makes the case and the system prioritizes.

The Problem

No front door for research. PMs and designers submitted requests through Slack DMs, emails, or copy-pasted Confluence templates. Requests got lost, duplicated, or forgotten. Every request felt "urgent" and researchers had no framework to prioritize or push back.

Product Managers
v1 — One Front Door
Airtable Form
→
Team Backlog

Built a simple Airtable intake form that auto-populates the team backlog. Strong adoption after all-hands launch. Requests stopped getting lost in Slack.

But researchers were still overwhelmed. Solved submission — not prioritization.
v2 — The Requestor Makes the Case

Instead of researchers justifying why something shouldn't happen, the requestor justifies why it should.

Urgency 1 – 5 + rationale
Form + Scoring
→

Autodesk AI-Powered Research Intelligence

Ask Me Anything

An AI-powered platform that searches across Dovetail, Prism, and internal repositories — so anyone can ask a question about what we know and get an answer in seconds.

The Problem

Autodesk has decades of deep, valuable research — and almost nobody can find it. Usability studies in Dovetail, archived research in Prism, customer data in PDMS, sentiment in UPF Hub. Each holds part of the picture, but they don't talk to each other.

× Research locked behind tool access — not everyone has Dovetail
× Hours spent manually searching and cross-referencing
× Teams commissioning new research on already-studied topics
× Quantitative and qualitative signals completely disconnected
What I Built

A full research intelligence platform that brings search, quotes, pain points, feature requests, dashboards, and alerts into the tools people already use — Slack, Confluence, and a Chrome extension. 2,450+ research items across 5 connected sources, searchable in seconds.

Platform Features
Cross-source Search

AI-generated summaries from Dovetail, Analytics, UPF Hub, and Prism with numbered citations and links to original sources.

Research Library

342 projects browsable by type, product, audience, and source. View counts, highlights, and tags for quick discovery.

Quote Library

124 searchable customer quotes across 38 studies. Copy, share to Slack, or cite — with source, segment, and sentiment filters.

Integrations — Meeting People Where They Work

The platform extends beyond the web app into the tools teams use every day. Slack bot for instant answers, topic alerts with semantic matching, a Confluence sidebar for contextual research, and a Chrome extension that surfaces related studies on any internal page.

Discovery Experience

The home page is designed for browsing, not just searching. Latest insights from across all sources, trending themes, recently added studies, and "most searched this week" — so teams can discover relevant research without knowing exactly what to ask for.

Expected Impact
2,450+ Research items searchable
5 Connected sources
89% Found what they needed
hrs → min Research discovery time
Democratized access — PMs, engineers, leadership all get research
Eliminated duplicate research across teams

Approach

How I think about
research operations

01

Journey-map first

Build from what people actually experience, not assumptions. Audit the existing system before designing in a vacuum.

02

Co-design for adoption

Adoption comes from ownership, not mandates. The people being measured should help define the metrics.

03

Standardize the scaffolding

Templates and pipelines, not prescriptive methods. Systems that support different research approaches without forcing one way.

04

Embed governance in the tool

When you don't have positional authority, design the governance into the system. Let the framework do the work.

05

Phase and iterate

Ship v1 fast to build trust. Layer on complexity once you know what people actually need — not what you assume they do.

06

Measure what matters

Adoption, time-to-insight, whether findings reach decisions. Does the system reduce burden — or just create more work?

How I measure success

Metrics

Adoption rates, participation, digest cycles, time-to-insight, research throughput

Governance

Consistent, scalable, not dependent on one person — playbooks that outlast you

Stakeholder satisfaction

Leadership sees value. Researchers see clarity. Partners trust the system.

The real test: does the system reduce burden and make research more impactful — or does it just create more work?
